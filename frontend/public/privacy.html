Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 25, 2026

Social Buster ("we," "us," or "our") operates the Social Buster platform at socialbuster.com, an AI-powered social media marketing tool. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information when you use our service.

By creating an account or connecting a social media platform, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

1. Information We Collect

1a. Account Information

  • Email address and password (password is hashed and stored by Supabase Auth — we never see or store your plaintext password)
  • Brand profile details you provide: brand name, industry, target audience, brand voice description

1b. Social Media Platform Data

When you connect a social media account (Facebook, Instagram, or other supported platforms), we collect:

  • OAuth access tokens — encrypted using AES-256-GCM before storage. Used solely to act on your behalf (publish posts, read comments, send messages).
  • Page/account identifiers — your Page ID and account name, used to route content to the correct destination.
  • Post engagement data — likes, comments, shares, reach, and impressions on posts you publish through our platform. Used to provide you with performance analytics.
  • Comments on your posts — text, author name, and timestamp of comments on posts published through Social Buster. Used to power the comment-to-DM automation feature you configure.
  • Direct message conversations — when you enable DM automation, we send messages on your behalf and store conversation state (message text, timestamps, and any information the recipient voluntarily provides such as email, phone number, or name). We only message users who have interacted with your content.

1c. Content You Create

  • Briefs, generated posts, captions, hashtags, and calls-to-action
  • Media files you select from connected cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, or Box) — media is not permanently stored on our servers. When you attach media to a post, a temporary copy is held in cloud-hosted storage (Supabase Storage) only for the duration of the publishing process. Once the post is published or deleted, the temporary copy is removed. We do not retain your images or videos on our infrastructure beyond what is needed to complete the publish.
  • AI-generated images created through the platform — stored in cloud-hosted storage (Supabase Storage), not on our application servers. You can delete these at any time from your media library.
  • Video clips you select from your media library — temporary processed copies are held in cloud-hosted storage during publishing, then removed.
  • DM automation rules you configure (trigger keywords, message templates, follow-up sequences)

1d. Billing Information

  • Subscription plan and status only. All payment processing is handled entirely by Stripe. Your credit card number, CVV, expiration date, and full billing address are entered directly on Stripe's secure payment form and are never transmitted to, processed by, or stored on Social Buster's servers. We only receive a confirmation of your subscription plan and payment status from Stripe — we never have access to your payment card details at any point.

1e. Usage and Log Data

  • Feature usage timestamps, API request logs, and error logs — used to maintain, debug, and improve the service.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Publish content — to create and publish posts to your connected social media accounts on your behalf, at the times you schedule.
  • Generate AI content — to produce post suggestions, captions, hashtags, and images based on the briefs and preferences you provide.
  • Monitor comments — to scan comments on your published posts for trigger keywords you define, so the DM automation you configured can respond.
  • Send direct messages — to send automated DMs on your behalf to users who comment on your posts with trigger phrases you set. Messages are only sent within each platform's allowed messaging window (24 hours for Meta platforms).
  • Collect leads — when a user responds to your automated DM with information (email, phone, name), we store that data in your account so you can export it.
  • Show performance analytics — to display engagement metrics, posting recommendations, and intelligence insights based on your own post performance.
  • Improve recommendations — we anonymously compile and compare performance metrics across users to improve AI-generated suggestions. For example, we calculate average engagement rates by industry, best posting times by region, and effective content formats by niche. This data is stripped of all identifying information before aggregation — your post content, brand name, account details, and personal information are never included. See Section 5 for full details on what is and is not included in aggregated data.
  • Communicate with you — to send account-related notifications, system alerts, and responses to support requests.

4. DM Automation and Messaging

Social Buster includes a comment-to-DM automation feature. Here is exactly how it works and what data is involved:

  1. You configure triggers — for each post, you define trigger keywords (e.g., "interested," "info") and the message(s) to send.
  2. We monitor comments — our system periodically checks comments on your published posts for your trigger keywords.
  3. We send DMs on your behalf — when a match is found, we send the message you wrote from your connected Page/account to the commenter. We respect each platform's 24-hour messaging window policy.
  4. Lead collection — if your automation includes follow-up questions (e.g., "What's your email?"), the recipient's responses are stored as lead data in your account. You can export this data as CSV.
  5. Rate limits — we enforce daily sending limits (100 DMs/day for Facebook, 80 DMs/day for Instagram) to protect your account from platform restrictions.

Recipient data: We store the commenter's platform user ID, name (as displayed on the platform), and any information they voluntarily provide in the DM conversation (email, phone, name, or custom fields you asked for). This data is only visible to you and is never shared with other Social Buster users or third parties.

5. Aggregated and Anonymized Data

We anonymously compile and compare performance metrics across users to improve AI-generated content recommendations for all users. This is strictly limited to numerical performance data and never includes personal information or content.

What IS included in aggregated data:

  • Average engagement rates (likes, comments, shares, reach) grouped by industry, platform, and post type
  • Best performing posting times grouped by niche and geographic region
  • Effective content formats and topics (e.g., "video posts in the fitness industry average 2x more engagement than image posts")
  • Average conversion rates for DM automations by industry (e.g., "retail brands see a 12% lead conversion rate")

What is NEVER included in aggregated data:

  • Your post content, captions, hooks, or hashtags
  • Your brand name, business name, or account name
  • Your email address or any personal contact information
  • Your media files (images, videos)
  • Your DM message content or lead data
  • Your OAuth tokens or platform credentials
  • Any information that could identify you or your business

This data is always aggregated and anonymized before it is used. No individual user's data can be identified or reverse-engineered from aggregated metrics. Aggregated data is never sold or shared with third parties — it is used solely to improve recommendations within the Social Buster platform.

6. Data Storage and Security

  • Database: Your data is stored in a PostgreSQL database hosted by Supabase (a cloud-hosted service), with Row-Level Security (RLS) policies ensuring each user can only access their own data. Your data is isolated from all other users at the database level.
  • Media files: Media (images, videos) is not stored on our application servers. Temporary copies are held in cloud-hosted storage (Supabase Storage) only during the publishing process. Once published or deleted, temporary media files are removed. We do not maintain a permanent copy of your media on our infrastructure.
  • OAuth tokens: All platform access tokens are encrypted using AES-256-GCM before storage. Encryption keys are stored separately from the database.
  • Passwords: Handled entirely by Supabase Auth using bcrypt hashing. We never store or have access to plaintext passwords.
  • Payments: All credit card numbers, CVV codes, expiration dates, and billing details are processed and stored exclusively by Stripe. No payment card information ever touches our servers. We only store your subscription plan name and payment status.
  • Personal data: Your personal information (email, brand details, post content, lead data, DM conversations) is never sold, rented, shared with, or made accessible to any third party, other Social Buster user, or external organization — except for the third-party services listed in Section 9 that are necessary to operate the platform, and only the minimum data required for each service.
  • Infrastructure: Our servers are hosted in secure data centers with standard industry protections (firewalls, access controls, encrypted connections via HTTPS).

7. Data Retention

  • Account data: Retained for as long as your account is active.
  • Post content and media: Retained until you delete it or delete your account.
  • DM conversations and leads: Retained until you delete them or delete your account.
  • Comments: Retained for up to 90 days for analytics purposes, then automatically purged.
  • Log data: Retained for up to 30 days, then automatically purged.
  • Aggregated metrics: Retained indefinitely as they contain no personally identifiable information.

8. User Data Deletion

You can request deletion of all your data at any time through either of these methods:

  • Email: Send an email to mark@markseminko.com with the subject line "Data Deletion Request." We will process your request within 30 days.
  • Platform revocation: You can revoke Social Buster's access to your Facebook/Instagram data at any time via Facebook Settings → Apps and Websites. This immediately revokes our access tokens — we can no longer access your platform data.

When you revoke access via Facebook/Instagram settings, we delete: DM conversations, collected lead data, DM automation configurations, connected platform tokens, and any direct messaging history. Your posts, briefs, media library, user profile, and billing data are preserved — these were created by you on our platform, not obtained via Meta's APIs.

Comment data: Comments on your posts are authored by third-party users (the people who commented), not by you. When you revoke access, we anonymize these comments by removing the commenter's handle, the platform comment ID, and the link to the original post. The comment text, sentiment classification, and platform remain as anonymous, de-identified data points used to improve our collective intelligence engine for all users. No individual commenter can be identified from this anonymized data.

Full account deletion: If you request full account deletion via email, we remove all data including your account, posts, briefs, media, and anonymized comment data.

Anonymized aggregated metrics (performance benchmarks, cohort averages) that have already been computed are not reversed, as they contain no identifying information.
